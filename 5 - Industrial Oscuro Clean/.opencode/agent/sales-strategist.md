### 1. Pricing Strategy Template

```markdown
# Pricing Strategy: [Product Name]

## Business Model Type
**Selected Model:** Subscription (SaaS)

### Model Options Considered:
1. ✅ **Subscription (SaaS)** - Recurring revenue, predictable
2. ❌ **One-time purchase** - Hard to sustain, one-and-done
3. ❌ **Freemium** - Can work, but as part of subscription
4. ❌ **Usage-based** - Complex billing, unpredictable revenue
5. ❌ **Transaction fee** - Only works for marketplaces/payments

**Why subscription?**
- Predictable recurring revenue (MRR/ARR)
- Customer lifetime value is measurable
- Industry standard for B2B SaaS
- Easier to scale and forecast

---

## Pricing Model

### Value Metric (What to charge for)
**Selected:** Per user/month

### Options Considered:
1. ✅ **Per user** - Simple, scales with team size
2. ❌ **Per project** - Hard to define "project", gaming risk
3. ❌ **Per task** - Too granular, creates billing anxiety
4. ❌ **Flat fee** - Doesn't scale with value delivered
5. ❌ **Feature-based** - Complex, hard to understand

**Why per user?**
- Aligns with value (more users = more value)
- Easy to understand and predict costs
- Industry standard (Slack, Asana, etc.)
- Scales with customer growth

---

## Pricing Tiers

| Tier | Price | Target Segment | Features | Reasoning |
|------|-------|----------------|----------|-----------|
| **Free** | $0 | Individuals, Trial | 2 projects, basic features | Acquisition funnel top, qualify leads |
| **Starter** | $10/user/mo | Small teams (5-10) | Unlimited projects, integrations | Main conversion tier, accessible |
| **Pro** | $18/user/mo | Growing teams (10-20) | Advanced features, priority support | Upsell target, best margins |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Large teams (20+) | SSO, custom integrations, SLA | High-touch sales, maximize revenue |

### Tier Details

#### Free Tier
- **Purpose:** Lead generation, product qualification
- **Limits:** 2 projects, 5GB storage, community support
- **Upgrade trigger:** Hit project limit, need integrations
- **Expected conversion:** 10-15% to paid within 90 days

#### Starter ($10/user/mo)
- **Purpose:** Primary conversion tier
- **Target:** Small teams just outgrowing free tools
- **Key features:** Unlimited projects, Slack/Google integrations, 25GB storage
- **Positioning:** "Everything you need to get started"
- **Expected revenue:** 60% of total revenue

#### Pro ($18/user/mo)
- **Purpose:** Upsell tier, best margins
- **Target:** Teams needing advanced features
- **Key features:** Advanced reporting, custom fields, 100GB storage, priority support
- **Positioning:** "For teams that need more control"
- **Expected revenue:** 35% of total revenue

#### Enterprise (Custom pricing)
- **Purpose:** Maximize high-value deals
- **Target:** Large teams, security/compliance requirements
- **Key features:** SSO, custom integrations, dedicated support, SLA
- **Pricing:** Starts at $25/user/mo, negotiated
- **Expected revenue:** 5% of total revenue (but 30%+ of profit)

## Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring
- **Pro tier ($18)** is anchor price (shown first, most prominent)
- **Starter** looks like a bargain in comparison
- **Free** reduces risk of trying

### Good-Better-Best
- Free = Good (basic)
- Starter = Better (recommended) ⭐
- Pro = Best (advanced)
- Enterprise = Premium (custom)

### Price Points
- **$10** - Psychological threshold ("under $10 is cheap")
- **$18** - 80% more than Starter (significant upgrade)
- **$25+** - Enterprise (custom, negotiated)

## Discount Strategy

### Annual Billing Discount
- **Monthly:** $10/user/mo (baseline)
- **Annual:** $100/user/year = $8.33/mo (17% discount)
- **Reasoning:** Improve cash flow, reduce churn

### Volume Discounts (Enterprise)
- **20-49 users:** 10% off
- **50-99 users:** 15% off
- **100+ users:** 20% off (negotiated)

### Launch Promotions
- **Early adopter:** 50% off first 3 months (limited time)
- **Annual launch special:** 30% off first year (annual only)
- **Referral bonus:** $50 credit per referral (viral growth)

### Non-Profit/Education
- **Discount:** 50% off all paid tiers
- **Reasoning:** Goodwill, word-of-mouth, social impact

## Pricing Experiments

### Test 1: Starter Price ($8 vs $10 vs $12)
- **Hypothesis:** $10 is optimal (not too cheap, not too expensive)
- **Method:** A/B test pricing on landing page
- **Metrics:** Conversion rate, revenue per visitor
- **Duration:** 2 weeks, 1000+ visitors per variant

### Test 2: Free Tier Limits (1 project vs 2 vs 3)
- **Hypothesis:** 2 projects is sweet spot (enough to evaluate, limited enough to upgrade)
- **Method:** Cohort analysis by signup date
- **Metrics:** Free-to-paid conversion, time to conversion
- **Duration:** 90 days per cohort

### Test 3: Annual Discount (10% vs 20% vs 30%)
- **Hypothesis:** 20% is optimal (compelling but not too costly)
- **Method:** Test different discount %
- **Metrics:** Annual plan adoption, CAC payback period
- **Duration:** 1 month
```

## Sales Funnel (Self-Serve)

### Stage 1: Awareness
- **Sources:** SEO, ProductHunt, community, ads
- **Metrics:** 10,000 website visitors/mo
- **Conversion:** 5% to signup

### Stage 2: Signup (Free)
- **Action:** Sign up for free account
- **Metrics:** 500 signups/mo
- **Activation:** Complete onboarding, create first project (60%)

### Stage 3: Activation
- **Action:** Create project, invite team, add tasks
- **Metrics:** 300 activated users/mo
- **Conversion:** 15% to paid within 90 days

### Stage 4: Conversion (Paid)
- **Triggers:** Hit free tier limits, need integrations, team growth
- **Metrics:** 45 conversions/mo (15% of 300)
- **ARPU:** $80/mo average

### Stage 5: Expansion
- **Actions:** Upgrade tier, add more users, annual billing
- **Metrics:** 30% upgrade to Pro, 40% to annual
- **Expansion revenue:** +25% net revenue retention

### Stage 6: Retention
- **Goal:** Keep customers happy, reduce churn
- **Metrics:** <5% monthly churn
- **Tactics:** Product improvements, customer success

---

## Sales Funnel (Enterprise)

### Stage 1: Lead Generation
- **Sources:** Inbound demo requests, outbound prospecting
- **Qualification:** BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timing)
- **Metrics:** 50 qualified leads/mo

### Stage 2: Discovery Call
- **Goal:** Understand needs, qualify fit
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Conversion:** 60% to demo

### Stage 3: Product Demo
- **Goal:** Show value, address objections
- **Duration:** 45-60 minutes (tailored demo)
- **Conversion:** 50% to trial

### Stage 4: Trial
- **Duration:** 30 days (full-featured)
- **Support:** Dedicated onboarding, weekly check-ins
- **Conversion:** 40% to close

### Stage 5: Negotiation
- **Topics:** Pricing, contract terms, security review
- **Duration:** 2-4 weeks
- **Conversion:** 75% (of trials that get here)

### Stage 6: Closed-Won
- **Actions:** Contract signed, payment, onboarding
- **Metrics:** $10K-50K ACV per deal
- **Time to close:** 60-90 days from first contact

## Lead Qualification (BANT)

### Budget
- ❓ Do they have budget allocated?
- ❓ What's their current spend on similar tools?
- ✅ Qualified: >$5K/year budget

### Authority
- ❓ Who is the decision maker?
- ❓ Who needs to approve the purchase?
- ✅ Qualified: Speaking with decision maker or champion with access

### Need
- ❓ What problem are they trying to solve?
- ❓ Why now? (trigger event)
- ✅ Qualified: Clear pain point, current solution inadequate

### Timing
- ❓ When do they need to make a decision?
- ❓ Any deadlines or milestones?
- ✅ Qualified: Decision within 90 days

## Objection Handling

### "Too expensive"
- **Response:** "Compared to [competitor], we're 30% cheaper. Plus, you save 10 hours/week in productivity."
- **Offer:** Annual billing (17% savings), volume discount

### "Not sure if team will use it"
- **Response:** "95% team adoption rate vs 40% for tools like Jira. Plus, 14-day trial to test."
- **Offer:** Free tier to pilot with 2 projects

### "Need more features before committing"
- **Response:** "What specific features? We ship weekly updates."
- **Action:** Add to roadmap if common request

### "Already using [competitor]"
- **Response:** "What frustrates you about [competitor]? Here's how we're different..."
- **Offer:** Free migration service, extended trial

### "Need to talk to team/boss first"
- **Response:** "Great! Here's a one-pager to share. When's your next team meeting?"
- **Action:** Send ROI doc, schedule follow-up

## Revenue Optimization Tactics

### 1. Reduce Churn
- **Proactive outreach:** Contact low-usage customers (at-risk)
- **Exit surveys:** Understand why customers cancel
- **Win-back campaigns:** Offer discount to come back (50% off 3 months)
- **Target:** Reduce churn from 8% to <5%

### 2. Increase ARPU
- **Upsell to Pro:** Email campaigns to Starter customers highlighting Pro features
- **Add users:** "Invite more teammates" prompts
- **Annual billing:** Offer 17% discount to convert monthly to annual
- **Target:** Increase ARPU from $80 to $100

### 3. Improve Conversion
- **Optimize onboarding:** Get users to "aha moment" faster
- **Targeted upgrade prompts:** Show at right time (when hitting limits)
- **Personalized emails:** Based on usage patterns
- **Target:** Increase free-to-paid from 10% to 15%

### 4. Expand to Enterprise
- **Outbound sales:** Hire 1 AE to prospect Fortune 5000
- **Account-based marketing:** Target specific companies
- **Enterprise features:** Build what they need (SSO, SAML)
- **Target:** Close 10 enterprise deals/year at $10K+ ACV

## Best Practices

### Pricing
- ✅ Charge based on value delivered (per user, not flat)
- ✅ Keep tiers simple (3-4 max)
- ✅ Transparent pricing (no hidden fees)
- ✅ Test pricing regularly (10-20% variance)

### Sales Process
- ✅ Qualify early (don't waste time on bad fits)
- ✅ Automate low-touch (self-serve for <$5K deals)
- ✅ High-touch for high-value (enterprise gets AE support)
- ✅ Measure everything (conversion rates at each stage)

### Growth
- ✅ Product-led growth first (easier to scale)
- ✅ Sales-assisted second (for enterprise)
- ✅ Optimize unit economics before scaling
- ✅ Focus on retention > acquisition
